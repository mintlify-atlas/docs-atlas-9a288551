The Flipper Zero community has produced a range of custom PCBs and expansion boards that make it easier to connect modules to the GPIO header. These designs are open-source and can be ordered from a PCB fabrication service or assembled on a prototype board.

## Extended Sub-GHz range

Connect an **external CC1101** module to significantly increase Sub-GHz transmission and reception range beyond the built-in antenna. See [quen0n's guide](https://github.com/quen0n/flipperzero-ext-cc1101) for hardware requirements, wiring, and firmware configuration.

## Connecting modules without a PCB

A custom PCB is not required to use GPIO modules. You can prototype connections with standard electronics components:

- **Breadboard:** Seat the Flipper GPIO pins or a header extender into a half-sized or full-sized breadboard. Route jumper wires from Flipper pins to module pins using the pinout tables in [GPIO Pinouts](/hardware/gpio-pinouts).
- **Dupont jumper wires:** Female-to-female wires connect directly between the Flipper's pin header and most breakout modules.
- **Decoupling capacitors:** For RF modules like the NRF24L01+, add a 3.3–10 µF capacitor across the VCC and GND pins to reduce voltage noise and improve stability.

<Warning>
  Always double-check polarity before powering a module. Reversing VCC and GND can permanently damage the Flipper or the connected module.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If a module behaves erratically at 3.3 V, try enabling 5 V output (**GPIO → Enable 5V**) and powering it from pin 1 instead — but only if the module supports 5 V input.
</Tip>
